Bonjour!


My name is Thanh-Hung Dang (you can call me "Hung", or "Hưng" in Vietnamese). I completed my PhD at Institute of Mathematics of Marseille , Aix-Marseille University, under the supervision of Alexis Bonnecaze and Stéphane Ballet.

In my PhD study, I worked on the topic related to "Efficient arithmetic in finite fields based on algebraic curves". My research interests lie in the area of Computational Number Theory; Cryptography, Coding Theory; Algorithms for finite field arithmetic.











News

  • 07 May 2021: A manuscript entitled "Optimization of the scalar complexity of Chudnovsky^2 multiplication algorithms in finite fields" has been accepted to Journal of Cryptography and Communications: Discrete Structures, Boolean Functions and Sequences (joint work with Alexis Bonnecaze and Stéphane Ballet).
  • 25 May 2020: I completed PhD dissertation defense at I2M, Luminy, Marseille.
  • 05 March 2020: I gave a talk updating some research results of my PhD thesis in the weekly seminar of ATI group at I2M.
  • 27-29 November 2019: I participated and presented my current research works in the RAIM 2019 conference of GdR Informatique Mathématique (GdR IM) organized at Institut de Mathématiques de Toulon, Université de Toulon, France.
  • 10-14 June 2019: I partipated in the 17th AGCT-2019 conference organized at CIRM, Marseille - Luminy, France.
  • 15 March 2019: A manuscript entitled "On the scalar complexity of original Chudnovsky^2 multiplication algorithm in finite fields " has been accepted to CAI 2019 (joint work with Alexis Bonnecaze and Stéphane Ballet).
